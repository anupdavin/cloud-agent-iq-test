§ 01 The forcing function

The dates are already on the calendar.

This isn't a "nice to have." Each of these deadlines has compliance, insurance, and audit consequences. Most enterprises haven't started.

  1. AUG 2025
    Already past

    Spring Boot 2.7 commercial support ended

    Every week since, applications still on Spring Boot 2.x accumulate unpatched CVEs. No security fixes. No backports. No official help.

  2. DEC 2025
    Already past

    Spring Boot 3.4 reached end of life

    Teams who upgraded to 3.4 thinking they were safe are now on an EOL version. Upgrade path: Spring Boot 3.5 (supported through June 2026) or jump to 4.x.

  3. NOW 2026
    You are here

    Spring Boot 4.0 shipped — breaking baseline

    New baselines: Jakarta EE 11, Tomcat 11, Hibernate 7.1, Servlet 6.1, Kotlin 2.2. Undertow no longer supported. Migrations now touch more of the stack than before.

  4. JUN 2026
    ~2 months out

    Spring Boot 3.5 reaches end of support

    The current default. Teams upgrading this quarter have a narrow window to land on a version that stays supported for more than six months.

  5. SEP 2026
    ~5 months out

    Oracle Java 11 reaches end of life

    24% of enterprise Java workloads still run on Java 11 (Azul, 2026 State of Java). Applications either migrate or move to a commercially supported OpenJDK distribution — fast.

  6. NOV 2026
    ~7 months out

    OpenJDK 8 reaches end of life

    21% of enterprises still run Java 8. Post-EOL applications fail SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, and GDPR audits. IBM puts the average breach cost at $4.88M.

Sources: Spring.io support policy updates · Azul 2026 State of Java · Oracle Java SE roadmap · IBM 2024 Cost of a Data Breach Report

§ 02 Deliverables

Three things on your desk at handover.

Not a slide deck. Not a status report. Things your engineers can use and your auditor can accept.

01

Modernized codebase

Your application on current LTS — typically Java 21 and Spring Boot 3.5+. Jakarta EE migrated. Hibernate updated. Test suite green. CI passing. Running in your environment, not a sandbox.

  • All direct and transitive dependencies on supported versions
  • Jakarta namespace migrated (javax.* → jakarta.*)

The thing your CISO and your auditor actually want. A signed artifact bundle that maps every dependency to its provenance, vulnerability status, and upgrade justification. Walks into SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA, and DORA audits without rework.

  • SBOM in CycloneDX and SPDX formats
  • Full CVE reconciliation — what was present, what's resolved
  • Dependency provenance & license inventory
  • Test evidence & regression coverage report
03

Migration runbook

Documented so your team can do the next one. Every decision we made, every workaround, every "here's what to watch for." Your second migration costs you less than your first — with or without us.

  • Step-by-step transformation log
  • Rollback procedure (tested, not theoretical)
  • Custom OpenRewrite recipes we authored for your codebase
  • Lessons learned & patterns for your other services
§ 03 The engagement

Three weeks. One application. No theatre.

Standard engagement shape. Larger portfolios get phased plans, but the per-application rhythm is the same.

  1. Week 01

    Audit & plan

    Read the code. Understand the load-bearing parts. Agree the target state.

    • Deep dependency scan & static analysis
    • Breaking-change inventory against target versions
    • Custom migration plan — sequenced, estimated, risk-rated
    • Written sign-off before any code changes
  2. Week 02

    Migration

    AI-accelerated refactor with senior human review on every change.

    • OpenRewrite recipes applied for deterministic changes
    • AI-assisted handling of the long-tail custom code
    • Daily PR stream — you review what you want to review
    • Test suite preserved & expanded where gaps exposed
  3. Week 03

    Validation & handover

    Prove it works. Document it. Hand your team the keys.

    • Full regression & integration test runs
    • Staging deployment & soak (your environment)
    • Evidence pack finalized & signed
    • Knowledge-transfer session with your engineers

Timelines assume a single-service Spring Boot application of moderate complexity. Multi-module, monolith, or legacy Java EE engagements are scoped after the audit.

§ 07 Real objections, real answers

Questions technical buyers actually ask.

What if you take longer than the agreed timeline?

Overrun is our problem, not your budget line. If we miss the committed date, we continue at our cost until the migration is complete. We price in contingency — you don't pay for ours.

What if you break production?

Migration work lands on a branch that only merges after your team approves every change. We test in your staging environment, not ours. The engagement includes a tested rollback procedure — if something's wrong post-deploy, you're never trapped on the new version. We've never broken production. We don't plan to start.

Why not just use our in-house engineers?

You can. Most of our clients could. The tradeoff is opportunity cost — a senior engineer spending three weeks on dependency upgrades is three weeks not spent on the roadmap. If the business cost of the delayed roadmap exceeds our fee, we're a buy. If not, you're right to do it internally.

Do you keep our source code?

No. Engagement is bound by an MSA with an NDA. Source access is revoked at handover. We don't retain copies. Any reusable tooling we build (custom OpenRewrite recipes, scripts) is handed to you with the runbook — we may abstract patterns for our own methodology, but never with your code.

How does AI factor into your delivery?

We use deterministic tools (OpenRewrite) for 70–80% of the transformation — rules-based, reviewable, no guesswork. AI assistants (Claude Code, Amazon Q, custom agents) handle the long tail of custom code that rules can't cover. Every AI-generated change is reviewed by a senior engineer before it lands. No "trust the model" — we trust the diff.

What's actually in the free audit?

You send us read access to a repository (or a pom.xml / build.gradle if that's easier). We spend 2 hours scanning dependencies, CVEs, EOL exposure, and breaking-change risk against current LTS. You get a written report: findings, risk-rated, with a fixed-price quote if you want us to do the migration. No pitch deck, no obligation.

Do you do non-Java stacks?

Not yet. We're deliberately narrow while we're two people — Java, Spring, JVM, Jakarta. Node, .NET, Python, Go migrations are in our roadmap but not our current offering. If you need one of those, we'll refer you to someone we trust.
